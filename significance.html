The Significance of an Eco-friendly Thriving Business

Businesses contribute not only to the country's economy but also effect the environment tremendously. Most businesses require a lot of materials to be put in, and they also produce a lot of material that goes out of the business (such as the materials they sell). All this material that is used to the make the product and is present in the product one day does end up being thrown away, contributing to accumulating waste. Our environment can be significantly harmed by waste that does not degrade properly. What's the solution to this? How can businesses make sure they are helping the environment and make sure they aren't contributing to the non-degrading waste? It's simple! Go green!

Going green is crucial aspect of innovation, sustanability, and contributions toward climate change. Ensuring that a material is biodegradable is important towards creating a healthier environment. Thus, an essential part of keeping planet Earth eco-friendly is biodegradability.

Biodegradable: the ability for a material to be broken down naturally by organisms in an ecosystem.


Biodegradable Materials

  • Paper
  • Bamboo
  • Cardboard
  • Leaves

Non-Biodegradable Materials

  • Plastic Bags & Packaging
  • Glass
  • Cans
  • Metals

Why are Non-Biodegradable harmful to the environment? Since these waste made of these materials will not decompose, it ends up becoming pollutants in the environment! Such waste usually ends up being on a portion of ground for a long time (soil) and will cause soil pollution. Sometimes, they are thrown into streams and they cause water pollution. Otherwise, and many times, they end up being burned, causing air pollution. Plastic is the most popular of the Non-Biodegradable materials.
